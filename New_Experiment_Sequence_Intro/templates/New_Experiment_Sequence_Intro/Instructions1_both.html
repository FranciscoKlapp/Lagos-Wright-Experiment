General Overview I - Sequences and Rounds
• Each participant will make decisions in a number of sequences

• Each sequence consists of an unknown number of rounds

• Each round consists of two parts: the Decentralized Goods Market and the Centralized Token Exchange

• At the end of each two-part round, a "virtual" die is rolled:
            → if the outcome is 1,2,3,4 or 5, then the sequence continues for round period
            → but if it lands on 6, then the sequence stops

That is, on average 5 out of 6 times -or 83.3% of the times- the sequence will keep going

• Once a sequence stops, if there is enough time a new sequence will start.
